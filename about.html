    entertainment

    Sheryl WuDunn, Joe Wong, and the Jubilee Project…all for an incredible cause
    This year’s benefit dinner features some of the most renowned and influential names in the Asian American community! Our keynote speaker, Sheryl WuDunn, is the first Asian woman to win the Pulitzer Prize. Her book, Half the Sky: Turning Oppression into Opportunity for Women Worldwide, and her movement of the same name, have been critical in fighting for women rights and equality. Award-winning stand-up comedian Joe Wong will enlighten us with his hilarious insights on American and Chinese culture. Finally, the Jubilee Project, which uses social media platforms and online videos to raise money for charity, will be showing us sneak peeks of their latest projects!


    our cause

    All proceeds from this year's benefit will go towards funding the operational costs of a Special Nursery Care unit at the China Care Home in Beijing. The nursery provides round-the-clock professional care for medically fragile orphaned infants both pre and post-surgery.Since we’ve begun funding this nursery unit in April 2009, the unit has provided care for over 60 orphaned babies.

    Harvard China Care is an independent, student-run organization of Harvard College that brings together a team of dedicated undergraduates determined to help build brighter futures for abandoned, orphaned, and special-needs children in China.

    Begun in 2003 by Gary Cooney '05, Harvard China Care is the first college club of the national nonprofit organization China Care Foundation. Since our founding, HCC has sent over 50 volunteers to China's orphanages and raised over $200,000 to fund surgeries, build a home for visually impaired orphans, and cover operational costs of children welfare homes in China.
